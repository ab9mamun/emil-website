title: "Effects of Patient-Driven Lifestyle Modification Using Intermittently Scanned Continuous Glucose Monitoring in Patients With Type 2 Diabetes: Results From the Randomized Open-label PDF Study"

summary: "This paper reports results from the randomized open-label PDF study, which evaluated whether intermittently scanned continuous glucose monitoring (isCGM) can support patient-driven lifestyle modification in people with type 2 diabetes. In the 12-week trial, 126 participants were assigned to either structured education with isCGM or standard care with blood glucose monitoring. The intervention used the SEOUL algorithm, a simple decision guide that helps patients adjust eating behavior based on whether a meal is generally healthy and whether it causes postprandial hyperglycemia. Compared with standard care, the isCGM-based intervention produced greater improvements in HbA1c, fasting blood glucose, body weight, and diabetes self-care behaviors, without severe hyperglycemia or hypoglycemia. Overall, the study shows that personalized, feedback-driven glucose monitoring can help patients better understand their own meal responses and make practical lifestyle changes to improve glycemic control."
