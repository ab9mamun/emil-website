summary: "This paper presents a real-world stress detection framework designed for Vessel Traffic Service (VTS) operators working in safety-critical maritime environments. Unlike many studies based on laboratory data, the authors collected long-term, on-site physiological data from VTS operators during routine operations, capturing real-world challenges such as individual differences, shift and sector variations, and imbalanced stress levels. The proposed model combines a cross-attention Transformer that integrates raw PPG signals with temporal and global HRV features, and a context-aware Mixture-of-Experts ensemble that adapts predictions based on operational context. The framework achieved 85.43% accuracy, 84.34% precision, 84.47% recall, and 84.50% F1-score, outperforming several existing machine learning and deep learning methods. The study also introduced a cross-subject and cross-day adaptation strategy, showing strong potential for personalized, wearable-based stress monitoring in real-world VTS and other safety-critical settings."
